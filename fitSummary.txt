Science: What?

	Infer the rules by which the universe works

		Processes

		Parameters

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Science: Why?

	Understanding

	Prediction

	Strategizing

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Science: How?

	We postulate possible rules and see how well they match:

		Existing data

		New data

	Why do we do it sort of backwards?

		Because directly inferring the rules is too hard

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Why new data?

	ANS To check the theory after we develop it

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Examples

	Newton

		You can't look at the planets and just tell how gravity works

		You guess how gravity works, then check and see what it explains

	Einstein

		Relativity was \emph{designed} to be consistent with known observations

		Needed to check by making weird new predictions

Statistics: What?

	Infer the rules by which a particular model works

		Mostly parameters

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Statistics: Why?

	Understanding

	Prediction

	Strategizing

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Statistics: How?

	We postulate possible parameters and see how well they match:

		Existing data

		New data

	Why (again)?

		Because directly inferring the parameters is too hard

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Why new data?

	ANS To avoid over-fitting (and over-confidence)

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Statistics

	A philosophy that allows us to make estimates and assess confidence based on
	quantitative data

		Frequentist

		Bayesian

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Likelihood

	How likely are data given parameters and assumptions?

	The tool that allows flexible evaluation of how well assumptions match data

	The foundation of both major statistical paradigms

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Summary

	Why: We fit models to understand what they can and can't tell us about the
	real world

	How: We use likelihood to fit models to data

		and to estimate uncertainty

	What to consider:

		Assumptions
